# Pvt Ltd Registration — Complete Document Checklist (2025)

## 👤 For Each Director & Shareholder (Minimum 2 needed)
The PAN card is the primary identity proof for all Indian directors and shareholders — the name, date of birth, and details must match across all submitted documents. Aadhaar is required as additional identity proof and must be linked to your mobile number for OTP-based DSC verification.

| Document | Details |
| :--- | :--- |
| **✅ PAN Card** | Mandatory for all Indian directors/shareholders |
| **✅ Aadhaar Card** | Must be mobile-linked for OTP verification |
| **✅ Passport-size photo** | Recent, white background, JPEG format |
| **✅ Address proof** | Any ONE: Bank statement / Electricity bill / Phone bill — not older than 2 months |
| **✅ Email ID & Mobile** | Personal (not shared between directors) |

---

## 🏠 Registered Office Documents
You must provide a valid registered office address along with proof and NOC from the property owner. You can use a residential address — MCA allows it. 

| Document | Details |
| :--- | :--- |
| **✅ Utility bill** | Electricity / water / gas bill — not older than 2 months |
| **✅ Rent agreement** | If office is rented |
| **✅ NOC from owner** | No-Objection Certificate from property owner |
| **✅ Sale deed** | If property is self-owned |

> 💡 **For shareholdervoting.in:** You can register using your home address in Chennai to start. You don't need a commercial office immediately.

---

## 📋 Statutory Documents (Drafted by a CS/CA)
You must gather all required documents including identity and address proof of directors, registered office proof, MOA, AOA, and declarations, then submit the SPICe+ form (INC-32) along with MOA (INC-33), AOA (INC-34), AGILE-PRO-S, and other supporting documents to the MCA portal.

| Document | What it is |
| :--- | :--- |
| **✅ MOA** | Memorandum of Association: Defines your company's objectives (must include "e-voting services") |
| **✅ AOA** | Articles of Association: Internal rules of the company |
| **✅ DSC** | Digital Signature Certificate: Class 3 DSC for each director — from govt-approved agency |
| **✅ DIN** | Director Identification Number: Applied via SPICe+ form automatically |
| **✅ Affidavit / Declaration** | INC-9 — Self-declaration by each director & subscriber |

---

## 🖥 The Registration Process (Step by Step)
The entire process covers: getting a Digital Signature Certificate, applying for Director Identification Number, name approval through the RUN service on the MCA portal, drafting and submitting MOA and AOA, filing incorporation forms with the Registrar of Companies, and receiving the Certificate of Incorporation. The whole process typically takes 10 to 15 working days.

* **Step 1 →** Reserve company name via MCA RUN portal (Suggested: "**ShareholderVoting Technologies Pvt Ltd**")
* **Step 2 →** Get Class 3 DSC for all directors (Cost: ₹1,500–₹3,000 per director)
* **Step 3 →** File SPICe+ Part A — Name approval
* **Step 4 →** File SPICe+ Part B — Full incorporation (Attach MOA, AOA, address proof, ID proof)
* **Step 5 →** MCA reviews → issues Certificate of Incorporation + CIN + PAN + TAN automatically
* **Step 6 →** Open current bank account within 180 days

## ⚠️ 3 Things Critical for ShareholderVoting.in

### 1. MOA must mention e-voting in the objects clause
The MOA's "Main Objects" section must explicitly state the core activity. 

**Suggested MOA "Main Objects" Clause for the CA/CS:**
> *"To carry on the business of providing, developing, managing, and operating electronic voting (e-voting) platforms, digital polling services, corporate governance technology solutions, and secure shareholder participation systems for companies, organizations, and institutions, as permitted under the Companies Act 2013, applicable SEBI regulations, and guidelines issued by the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA) or other regulatory authorities in India and abroad."*

*(This broad wording ensures you are covered for STQC certification and MCA/SEBI compliance.)*

### 2. Authorised capital — start at ₹10 lakhs
Authorised capital can start as low as ₹1,000, and there are no ROC fees up to ₹10 lakhs. For an e-voting platform seeking STQC certification, having ₹10L authorised capital looks much more credible to regulators and clients.

### 3. At least one director must be a resident Indian
At least one director must be an Indian resident — someone who stays in India for 182 or more days in the previous calendar year. Since you're operating from Chennai, this requirement is naturally fulfilled.
